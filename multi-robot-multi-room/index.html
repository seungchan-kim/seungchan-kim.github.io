Multi-Robot Multi-Room Exploration with
Structural Cue Extraction and Sphere Representations

Abstract

This work proposes an autonomous multi-robot exploration pipeline that coordinates the behaviors of robots in an indoor environment composed of multiple rooms. Contrary to simple frontier-based exploration approaches, we aim to enable robots to explore multiple rooms one-by-one in a order, keep track of which rooms are already explored or being occupied, and share these information among robots to coordinate their behaviors in a distributed fashion. To this end, we propose (1) geometric cue extraction method that processes 3D map point cloud data and detects the locations of potential cues such as doors and rooms, (2) sphere representations for convex spaces (e.g. rooms) used for local viewpoints generation and efficient communication of information. Using this two components, our pipeline effectively assigns the exploration tasks among robots with more context and situational awareness. We evaluate the performance of our pipeline using a team of aerial robots, both in simulation and real-world experiments.
